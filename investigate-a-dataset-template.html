Introduction¶

The dataset for this investigation is the No-show appointments dataset. It is based on informataion collected from more than 100 thousand medical appointments in brasil and based on gathering data on weather patients showed up for appointments or not. Several variables are used to capture the appropriate data to dissect the question on weather a patient will show up for appointments or not.

In our attempt to understand the insight of the data, we shall focuse on just two questions that goes thus;

  1. Does being an alcoholic influence ones likelihood of showing up for hospital appointments?
  2. Do all those who receive SMSs show up for appointments? and is there any association between receiving an SMS and showing up for appointments?

A chi square test can be used to determine association between two categorical variables. At the 95% confidence level, it is observed that no association exist as the p value is greater than 0.05(0.965).

In [60]:

Conclusions¶

Regarding research question 1, it can be speculatively observed that more persons showed up for appointments both in categories of those who were alcoholic and were not. This visibly meant that alcoholism might not affect ones likelihood of showing up for appointments. Though not required in this analysis, further statistical tests(Chi-square) were carried out to ascertain these results and it was confirmed that no statistical associations exist between alcoholism and Ones likelihood of showing up for appointments (p>0.05) hence we can safetly conclude that alcoholism does not influence ones likelihood of showing up for appointments.
Regarding research question 2, it was seen that among those who received SMSs, not all of them showed up for appointments as 23.3% showed up while 8.9% did not show up.
Though not required, following on to asses the statistical association between the reception of SMSs and showing up for appointments, a statistically significant association was obserced at the 95% confidence level as the p value was less than 0.05(0.0). This meant the data provided enough evidence for us to confirm that ones reception of an SMS can influence their showing up for appointments or not.

Limitations to the study¶

  1. The dataset had certain challenges associated with coding as there was no adeqaute information from the data description of variable values. This could lead to misinterpretation and misrepresentation of data. This is visbly seen in the Handcap variable in which several categories originated and as a result the variable had to be ultimately droped. This could be avoided by adding an appropriate coding section for the data.
  2. Per the original demand of the analysis, using only descriptives is not sufficient to determine statistical associations, hence following this original structure, our analysis would have provided only tentative results. This was however handled as we used the Chi-squared test in our analysis.
